# The Rapist's Endorsement

## A Polish MEP Believes Ireland's Only Hope Is a Man Civilly Liable for Rape

On June 14, 2026, Polish Member of the European Parliament Dominik Tarczyński posted a video on X with a simple caption: **"ONLY THIS MAN CAN SAVE IRELAND."**

The man in question: Conor McGregor.

The same Conor McGregor who, on November 22, 2024, was found civilly liable for raping Nikita Hand in a Dublin hotel room — an assault so brutal that a tampon she was wearing had to be surgically removed by a doctor using forceps.

This is not satire. This is the current state of European politics.

---

## The Verdict

On December 9, 2018, Nikita Hand attended a Christmas party at the Beacon Hotel in Dublin. She knew Conor McGregor. They had mutual friends. She was on her period and wearing a tampon. She told him she could not have sex with him.

What happened next was described in detail under oath at the High Court in Dublin.

Hand testified that McGregor isolated her in a bedroom, choked her, and raped her. The next morning, she went to the Rotunda Hospital sexual assault unit. Dr. Daniel Kane, a gynaecologist and forensic examiner, described the tampon as being **"wedged" at the "very very top" of her vagina** — so deeply lodged that he had to use forceps to remove it. He found a possible internal tear and **"a multiplicity of injuries"** including bruising on all four limbs, her knuckles, and her buttocks. He classified the injuries as **"moderate to severe."**

A paramedic who examined Hand noted her primary concern was that the tampon had been **"pushed up way too far."**

On November 22, 2024, an Irish jury found McGregor civilly liable for the assault. He was ordered to pay damages. He was never criminally charged — a fact his defenders have wielded ever since. But the civil standard — balance of probabilities — was met. Twelve Irish citizens heard the evidence and concluded: this happened.

## The Politician

Dominik Tarczyński is a Polish MEP for the Law and Justice (PiS) party. Born in 1979, he served in Poland's Sejm from 2015 to 2020 before moving to the European Parliament. He is a fixture of Europe's hard-right ecosystem: anti-immigration, anti-Islam, pro-border, pro-Trump.

His recent X output tells the story. On June 13, 2026, he posted: **"Deport! Deport! Deport the fucking lot!"** In May 2026, he was banned from entering the United Kingdom to attend a Tommy Robinson rally — a ban he threatened to sue over, calling it "communism in the 21st century." He has appeared on podcasts titled "Not One Muslim."

Tarczyński is not some anonymous internet troll. He is an elected representative of the European Union's second-largest member state, drawing a salary funded by European taxpayers, with a platform that reaches hundreds of thousands.

And he looked at a man found liable for rape — a man whose victim needed surgical intervention to remove a tampon forced inside her during the assault — and declared him **Ireland's only hope.**

## The Context

McGregor's pivot to politics is not new. Since 2024, he has become an increasingly vocal anti-immigration figure in Irish public life. He has called for Ireland to **"close the borders"** to keep "mentally deranged people out." He announced a presidential bid in 2025 — which he later abandoned, claiming the process was "fixed." He has met with Donald Trump at the White House and has been photographed with far-right figures across Europe.

The logic is familiar: take a celebrity with name recognition, add grievance politics about immigration, and watch the far-right infrastructure snap into place. It worked for Trump. It worked for various figures across Europe. Why not for a man who made his fortune beating people unconscious in an octagon?

What makes the Tarczyński endorsement extraordinary is not that it happened — it is that it happened so openly, so shamelessly, with the rape verdict mattering so little that it apparently never crossed Tarczyński's mind as a disqualifier.

## The Lie

There is a lie embedded in the frame, and it is not about whether McGregor committed the assault. The jury spoke on that. The lie is in the premise: that Ireland needs "saving" at all.

Ireland is a stable democracy with a functioning legal system that — unlike many others — allowed a woman to sue a powerful man and win. That is not a country in need of rescue by a rapist. That is a country functioning exactly as it should.

The "Ireland needs saving" narrative is the same script used across Europe: invent a crisis, position a strongman as the solution, and treat every institution that would hold him accountable as part of the conspiracy. The courts that found McGregor liable? Corrupt. The journalists who reported it? Biased. The woman who testified under oath? A liar.

Tarczyński, a Polish politician, has no electoral stake in Ireland. He cannot vote there, cannot campaign there, cannot legally affect Irish policy. His endorsement is not politics — it is propaganda. It is an attempt to normalize the frame that a man found liable for rape is a legitimate political leader, and that the women who hold him accountable are obstacles to "saving" the nation.

---

## The Silence

What is missing from Tarczyński's post is as telling as what is present.

There is no mention of Nikita Hand. No mention of the forceps. No mention of the tampon wedged at the top of a woman's vagina after she said no. No mention of the bruises on four limbs or the internal tear or the paramedic's report.

There is only the video. The strongman. The promise of salvation.

This is how the architecture works: remove the victim from the frame, remove the evidence from the frame, remove the law from the frame, and what remains is a man and a flag and a promise that someone else will pay the price for your grievance.

Tarczyński believes that the man who did that to Nikita Hand is Ireland's only hope.

The terrifying thing is: he may not even see the contradiction.
